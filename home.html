Overview

IS 201 covers a lot of material, but here are the big things I gained/learned this semster!

  1. Proficiency in Essential Technology Tools and Applications
    • Gained hands-on experience with essential software applications such as Microsoft Excel, Solver, and VBA (Visual Basic for Applications), used for data analysis, modeling, and automation.
    • Acquired skills in managing databases, including data organization, querying, and manipulation, to extract meaningful insights from large datasets.
    • Developed proficiency in web development using HTML (Hypertext Markup Language), enabling the creation of basic web pages and understanding the structure of web content.
  2. Understanding Information Systems in Organizational Context
    • Explored the role of information systems in organizations, understanding how they support business operations, decision-making, and strategic planning.
    • Gained insights into the lifecycle of information systems development, from requirements gathering and analysis to implementation and maintenance.
    • Learned how to leverage technology to enhance organizational efficiency, productivity, and competitiveness, by aligning information systems with business objectives and processes.
  3. Harnessing Business Intelligence through Data Mining and Visualization
    • Developed skills in data mining techniques to discover patterns, trends, and relationships within large datasets, using tools like Data.World and other data mining applications.
    • Learned how to visualize data effectively using tools like Data.World, Excel, and other data visualization applications, to communicate insights and findings in a clear and compelling manner.
    • Understood the importance of business intelligence in decision-making, and how organizations can use data-driven insights to gain a competitive edge, optimize operations, and identify new opportunities for growth.
